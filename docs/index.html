Redaction Before Delegation: Benchmarking Local PII Filters on Consumer-GPU-Class Hardware

Sveinn Pálsson
Technical report / preprint · May 17, 2026

PDF: redaction-before-delegation.pdf

Code and data: github.com/sveinnpalsson/pii-redaction-eval

Abstract

Cloud-hosted language models and retrieval systems are increasingly used with private text from documents, notes, logs, issue trackers, and local knowledge stores. A practical mitigation is to redact personally identifiable information (PII) locally before text crosses an application or model-provider trust boundary. We benchmark local PII and sensitive-span filtering architectures on consumer-GPU-class hardware, comparing deterministic rules, Presidio, OpenAI Privacy Filter (OPF), OPF plus deterministic safety rules, and local decoder LLM redaction. On an AI4Privacy-derived validation subset with 1,065 cases and 4,201 expected sensitive spans, OpenAI Privacy Filter leaves 444 visible spans at roughly 20 ms per case; adding deterministic safety rules reduces visible leakage to 395 spans with additional over-redaction; Qwen3.5-27B direct local redaction leaves 284 visible spans but requires roughly 3.7 s per case. On a synthetic 200-case local-workspace stress set, OPF plus deterministic safety rules substantially improves coverage of file paths, handles, account-like identifiers, URLs, and secret-like strings, while the local decoder remains the highest-capacity but slowest comparator. The results suggest that specialized local token-classification redactors are the strongest low-latency baseline for standard PII redaction. Local decoder LLMs reduce residual leakage in some settings and offer prompt-level policy flexibility, but at substantially higher latency and memory cost. The benchmark therefore reports visible leakage, wrong-label hides, over-redaction, latency, peak GPU memory, taxonomy mismatch, uncertainty, and reproducibility metadata alongside aggregate F1.
